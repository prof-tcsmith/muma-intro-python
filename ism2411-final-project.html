Final Project Guidelines: Python for Business Analytics

Project Overview

The final project for this Python for Business course is designed to demonstrate your mastery of Python programming concepts and their application to real-world business problems. You will develop an end-to-end data analysis solution that showcases your ability to work with business data, extract meaningful insights, and present those insights effectively.

Team Formation

  • Students may work individually or in teams of 2-3 members

  • Team members should clearly define responsibilities and contributions

  • Teams must notify the instructor of their composition by the end of Module 8

  • Individual projects are assessed with the same rigor as team projects, with expectations adjusted accordingly

Project Requirements

Core Components

Your project must incorporate the following components covered throughout the course: 1. Data Acquisition and Cleaning

  • Import data from external sources (CSV, Excel, or other formats)

  • Implement data validation and cleaning procedures

  • Document data quality issues and their resolution

  • Handle missing values appropriately

  1. Data Analysis and Manipulation

    • Apply appropriate data transformations using Pandas

    • Implement at least three different aggregation or grouping operations

    • Create calculated fields or derived business metrics

    • Use functions to modularize analytical operations

  2. Data Visualization

    • Create at least four different types of visualizations using Matplotlib

    • Include appropriate titles, labels, and legends

    • Ensure visualizations effectively communicate business insights

    • Format visualizations professionally for business presentations

  3. Business Logic Implementation

    • Use conditional statements and control structures to implement business rules

    • Create at least two reusable functions that perform business calculations

    • Implement error handling for potential exceptions

    • Document business logic with clear comments and documentation

  4. Reporting and Interpretation

    • Generate formatted output suitable for business stakeholders

    • Provide clear interpretations of analytical findings

    • Include actionable recommendations based on your analysis

    • Address limitations of your analysis and potential next steps

Project Types

Choose one of the following project types or propose your own (subject to instructor approval):

  1. Market Analysis

    • Analyze sales data across regions, product categories, or time periods

    • Identify trends, seasonality, and growth opportunities

    • Segment customers or products based on performance metrics

    • Recommend strategies for improving market position

  2. Financial Performance Analysis

    • Analyze revenue, costs, and profitability across business units

    • Identify factors driving financial performance

    • Forecast future performance based on historical trends

    • Recommend strategies for improving financial outcomes

  3. Operational Efficiency

    • Analyze process data to identify bottlenecks or inefficiencies

    • Measure key performance indicators (KPIs) for operational processes

    • Model potential process improvements and their impact

    • Recommend strategies for improving operational efficiency

  4. Customer Analytics

    • Analyze customer behavior, preferences, or satisfaction

    • Segment customers based on various attributes or behaviors

    • Identify opportunities for improved customer experience

    • Recommend strategies for increasing customer retention or acquisition

  5. Custom Project

    • Propose a custom project that aligns with your interests

    • Must be approved by the instructor by the end of Module 9

    • Should be comparable in scope and complexity to the options above

Deliverables

Project Proposal (Due after Module 9)

Submit a 1-2 page proposal that includes: - Project title and type

  • Team members and their roles (if applicable)

  • Description of the business problem being addressed

  • Data sources and brief description of the dataset(s)

  • Preliminary analysis plan and expected outcomes

  • Timeline for completion

Final Code Repository

Submit a complete Thonny project folder containing: - Well-commented Python scripts (.py files)

  • Data files used in the analysis (or clear instructions for accessing them)

  • README file explaining the project structure and execution instructions

  • Requirements or dependencies documentation

Project Report (Due within four days after the final class)

Submit a comprehensive report (8-12 pages for teams, 5-8 pages for individuals) including: 1. Executive Summary (1 page)

  • Business problem and objectives

  • Key findings and recommendations

  • Expected business impact

  1. Introduction (1-2 pages)

    • Detailed problem statement

    • Business context and significance

    • Project scope and limitations

  2. Methodology (2-3 pages)

    • Data sources and description

    • Data preparation and cleaning processes

    • Analysis techniques and justification

    • Technical approach and implementation details

  3. Results and Analysis (3-4 pages)

    • Key findings with supporting visualizations

    • Interpretations of analytical results

    • Business implications of findings

  4. Recommendations (1-2 pages)

    • Data-driven recommendations

    • Implementation considerations

    • Expected outcomes and potential ROI

  5. Appendices (as needed)

    • Technical documentation

    • Additional visualizations

    • Data dictionary

    • Code snippets (key sections only, not the entire codebase)

Presentation (10-15 minutes, during Module 12)

Prepare a professional presentation that includes: - Introduction to the business problem

  • Overview of your approach

  • Key findings and visualizations

  • Recommendations and business impact

  • Technical challenges and solutions

  • Demonstration of your Python application (optional)

  • Q&A session

Peer Evaluations (For team projects only)

Each team member must complete a confidential peer evaluation form assessing: - Contribution to the project (code, analysis, report writing)

  • Quality of work

  • Communication and collaboration

  • Meeting deadlines and reliability

  • Overall assessment of team dynamics

Assessment Criteria

Projects will be evaluated based on:

  1. Technical Proficiency (40%)

    • Correct implementation of Python concepts

    • Code quality, efficiency, and organization

    • Appropriate use of libraries and functions

    • Error handling and robustness

  2. Business Insight (25%)

    • Depth of business understanding

    • Quality of analysis and interpretation

    • Relevance and feasibility of recommendations

    • Connection between data and business outcomes

  3. Presentation and Communication (20%)

    • Report clarity and organization

    • Quality of visualizations

    • Professional presentation delivery

    • Ability to answer questions and defend approach

  4. Project Management (15%)

    • Meeting deadlines

    • Scope management

    • For team projects: effective collaboration

    • Documentation quality

Timeline

  • Module 9: Project introduction and discussion

  • End of Module 9: Project proposals due

  • Module 10-11: Project work with optional check-ins

  • Module 12: In-class presentations

  • Four days after Module 12: Final report and code submission

Resources and Support

  • Office hours dedicated to project support will be scheduled throughout Modules 10-12

  • Sample datasets will be provided for students who don’t have access to business data

  • A project FAQ document will be available on the course website

  • Technical troubleshooting sessions can be arranged by appointment

Academic Integrity

  • All data sources must be properly cited

  • Code borrowed or adapted from other sources must be acknowledged

  • Each team member must contribute substantially to the project

  • The use of AI tools for code assistance is permitted but must be disclosed and explained

This final project is your opportunity to demonstrate your Python skills in a business context and create a portfolio piece that showcases your ability to derive actionable insights from data.
